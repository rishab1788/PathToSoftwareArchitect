In this lesson, you will learn to identify and use business idioms related to decision-making. Let's start with a little story... 

How many idioms can you find?


1 Give it some thought 💭
Definition: Think carefully about something.
Example: "This is a big decision, so let me give it some thought, and I will call you.”

2. Have second thoughts 🤔
Definition: Have doubts about something.
Example: "At first, it seemed like a great idea, but now I’m having second thoughts."

3 A no-brainer 🧠
Definition: A very easy or obvious choice, decision, or solution.
Example: "If the newer version performs as well for half the cost, the decision is a no-brainer.”

4. Sleep on it 😴
Definition: Delay making a decision on something until the following day.
Example: "Let me sleep on it and give you an answer tomorrow.”


5. Make a snap decision 🙌
Definition: Make a decision too quickly, without thinking too much.
Example: "The issue was urgent, so I had to make a snap decision."

6. Be in two minds 🤕 🤕
Definition: Be unable to decide between alternatives.
Example: "I’m in two minds about whether to accept their proposal or not."


7. On the fence 🤷🏼‍♂️
Definition: Undecided between two options.
Example: " I’m on the fence about whether to accept their proposal or not."

8. Make up my mind ☝️
Definition: Make a final decision.
Example: "You need to make up your mind as soon as possible."

9. Change your mind 🙊
Definition: Change your original opinion.
Example: "He changed his mind and decided to go for the cheapest option."
