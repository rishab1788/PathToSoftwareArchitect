
### Structure your opinion effectively.
### Support your opinion with evidence or examples.
### Use appropriate language and tone.

1. State your opinion:
I think
I believe
I feel 
In my opinion
In my view
I’m fairly confident that
If you ask me
I could be wrong, but I think
All things considered, I think that
I have the impression that
It seems to me that
2. Give reasons or evidence:
You can support your opinion with facts, examples, and your personal experience. Here are some phrases you can use:

Research shows...
According to the data...
This is based on...
For instance
For example
From my experience...
I've found that in my previous role...

3. Summarize or conclude:
So, in conclusion...
To sum up...
So, in a nutshell...

In a nutshell
An idiomatic expression used to describe summarizing something in a clear and concise manner. It means to say something in just a few words.




What do you think?
Look at the following questions and think about how you would reply to them using the phrases above.

💡 Challenge yourself! Try not to use "I think" and practice new phrases.

In your opinion, is it more enjoyable to read books or watch movies?
What do you think are some common stereotypes for your country? What are your thoughts?
In what way does advertising influence what we buy?
