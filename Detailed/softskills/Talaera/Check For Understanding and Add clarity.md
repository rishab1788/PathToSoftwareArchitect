paraphrasing

Paraphrasing is the act of repeating the same idea using different words.
Paraphrasing has many benefits! Here are a few of them: 

Synonym
Use words with similar meanings.

Word category
Use different word types (actions, nouns...).

Summary
Condense the main points.

Register
Change the level of formality.

Structure
Organize content and sentences differently.


Change the word to another one with a similar meaning. 

There has been a drop in sales. → There has been a decline in sales.
Can we tweak our goal? → Can we adjust our objective?
What sort of error are we looking for? → What type of error are we looking for?





Original: While we suffered a dismal first quarter because of supply-chain issues, and our stock price wobbled a lot thanks to fluctuations in the Japanese market that caused the company to begin targeting employees for layoffs, the executive team is happy to report that we are on track for a profitable year. 
Summary: The executive team is happy to report that we are on track for a profitable year, even though we suffered a hard first quarter. 


Change the register

Change the register
Register refers to how formal or informal the message is. Usually, we adapt the register to the situation and the people we are interacting with. With coworkers, we tend to communicate in a more neutral-informal way, while with high-level investors we might adopt a more formal register. Although during a conversation we usually do not switch registers, it is a helpful skill to be able to adapt and vary from more informal to more formal and it can help you with effective communication.

Formal: I apologize but I need to postpone our meeting.
Informal: Hey man, I can’t make it. Let’s do it another time.
