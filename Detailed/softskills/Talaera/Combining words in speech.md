Apostrophes: Possession vs. contractions
What is the difference between using apostrophes to indicate possession versus using apostrophes in contractions? 

Contractions - apostrophes replace the letter or letters that have been omitted. 

They have paid --> They've paid
It has been fun --> It's been fun
Possessives - apostrophes indicate ownership or possession. 

When a singular noun is in possession of something: add 's and when a plural noun is in possession: add '

Madhusha's car is red.
The managers' lounge is on the fifth floor. 
*Exception*

It's is the contraction of it is or it has
Its is a possessive determiner that indicates that something belongs to the subject: it
