What will you learn?
Learn 6 strategies for communicating your ideas effectively:

Use direct, active language.
Use simple words.
Include context.
Describe steps.
Remove non-essential information.
Move forward when you get stuck.


Essentials of effective communication
Effective communication means:

 Being heard.
 Being clear.
 Checking for understanding.
 Using more than words to share a message. 
 Getting people to do what is needed.


You can compare effective communication to fishing.

Casting the fishing rod 
This is your opening, where you capture attention. Think of it as throwing out a line and hoping someone bites.


Baiting the hook
Once you have attention, you need to be understood. Provide meaningful information that resonates, and your audience will 'bite' into your message.

Reeling in the fish
This final step involves inspiring action. Whether you want them to laugh, buy your product, or support your idea, this is where persuasion comes into play.


Express clear ideas with confidence
1. Easy words
Avoid technical or complex words. Choose the easier version ("use" instead of "utilize").

❌ Kindly transmit the comprehensive document to my attention no later than the close of business on the upcoming Friday.
✅ Please send me the report by Friday.
2. Active voice
Structure sentences so the subject performs the action and appears at the beginning (start with WHO + ACTION).

❌ The project was completed ahead of schedule by the team.
✅ The team completed the project ahead of schedule.


1. Use simple language
Use active voice and easy words and avoid jargon. Common, simple language is more effective!

2. Provide context
Explain the situation with short sentences.

3. Use steps
Describe the process or your idea in simple, sequential steps (part 1, part 2, part 3...).

4. Include (only) important details
Include important, specific details. And also eliminate unnecessary information that doesn't contribute to the main point.


1. Use simple language:
Instead of saying, "We're migrating to an enterprise-grade password management solution to enhance our security protocols," the IT manager says:

✅ Simple Language: "We are changing to a new system to keep our passwords safe."

2. Provide context:
The IT manager further explains the situation:

✅ Context: "Our old way of storing passwords isn't as secure as it could be. This new system will help protect our information better, and it's important for everyone to use it."

3. Use steps:
The IT manager explains with clear, sequential steps:

✅ Step 1: "First, you'll need to log into the new system using your existing email and password."
✅ Step 2: "Next, you'll be asked to create a new, strong password that you haven't used before."
✅ Step 3: "Finally, save the new password and make sure to use this system whenever you need to access protected company files."

4. Include (only) important details:
The IT manager includes important details that are essential for the team to know and excludes unnecessary technical information:

✅ Important Details: "Remember, your new password should be at least 12 characters long and include numbers, letters, and special symbols. This helps make it strong. And don't worry, if you forget it, there's a 'Forgot Password' option to help you reset it."
