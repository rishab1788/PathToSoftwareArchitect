How you will use this lesson at work

The beginning of a meeting is more important than one may think. Strong openings are the key to productive meetings.
Starting a meeting effectively helps you set the tone, introduce the main topic, set expectations, and make a good first impression.
By the end of this lesson, you'll be able to kickstart any meeting with energy, clarity, and confidence. Let’s get to it!

1️⃣ Welcome participants

If everyone is present, you can use these phrases:
Alright, it seems we're all here. I see we're all set and ready to go. 
Thank you for taking the time to meet today.
Thank you for joining.
Since everyone is here, we should get started.
I really appreciate you all for attending this meeting today.
Thank you for joining us today. It's great to see you all.

Phrases for when you need to start, even though not everyone has joined yet:

While we're waiting for a few more to join, let's get started with...
It looks like some folks are still trying to finish their previous meeting, but we have a full agenda. So, let's begin with...
A few team members are running late, but they've given us the green light to start without them. So, let's dive into X.
Some participants might be joining us shortly. While we wait, we can...
We have a packed agenda, so let's begin. We'll make sure to loop in those who join us later.
I know some are still on their way, but let's make a start. They can jump in when they arrive.

2️⃣ Share the context of the meeting
Setting the context for a meeting is essential as it gives everyone a clear understanding of the purpose and ensures that participants are engaged. Here are some more phrases you can use to introduce the reason for the meeting:

The purpose of today's meeting is to...
This meeting has been arranged to...
Our aim today is to...
Our main goal today is to...
This meeting is focused on...
Today's meeting has been called to address...
We've gathered here to...
I've invited you all here today to align our efforts and...
Use phrases like these to create an environment where participants understand the 'why' behind the meeting, encouraging more active participation and engagement. It's a great way to ensure that everyone is on the same page from the start!




Be specific about goals
Set very clear objectives and state the type of action items you would like to define by the end of the meeting. Be specific about the purpose of each agenda item and tell them why you are meeting and what you want to get out of it. You will want to use verbs and action words for this section (generate ideas, find a solution, decide the budget).

❌ We'll talk about the budget.
✅ We'll decide the budget allocation for our Q4 marketing campaign.
❌ We'll discuss the project.
✅ We'll outline the project's next steps and assign responsibilities.



4️⃣ Start the meeting!
 And now it's time to finally get a meeting started. You can use the phrases below:

Alright, let's get the ball rolling!
Let's get down to business. 
Today, we'll start off by looking at...
I'll begin by walking you through...
May I start off with...
So, let's begin with...
First, I'd like to show you...
Great, let's get to it!
