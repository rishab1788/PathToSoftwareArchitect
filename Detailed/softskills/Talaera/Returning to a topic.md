Have you ever found yourself in a conversation that's gone off the rails? You know, that moment when everyone's talking about the company picnic and you're still thinking about the budget spreadsheet? Circling back to an earlier point is an important skill in meetings. It keeps the conversation focused and ensures key issues are addressed. In this lesson, you will learn to:

Recognize the importance of returning to topics in discussions.
Learn various expressions to circle back.
Practice using these expressions in real-life scenarios.

Why circle back?
Imagine you're in a meeting discussing a critical project. 
The conversation drifts into last weekend's sports events. 
How do you refocus everyone? Circling back!

Why circle back?

Focus: Keeps discussions on target.
Clarity: Ensures all vital topics are covered.
Efficiency: Prevents wasting time on tangents.


Circle back

To circle back means to return to a topic or issue at a later time. For example, after discussing the marketing strategies, the manager may say, 'Let's circle back to the budget concerns after lunch.'


Expressions to circle back
Read the following expressions aloud to practice returning to a previous point and clarify it or add more information. 

Could we circle back to...?
Can we return to that topic?
I'd like to follow up on...
Is it alright if we discuss this topic further?
As I was saying earlier...
Can we take another look at...?
This ties back to my previous comment...


"Could we circle back to the marketing strategy? I want to ensure we're aligned on our goals."

Thank you, Liam. Let's go back to our new product idea for a second and we'll address the last project's questions afterward. Is that OK?
